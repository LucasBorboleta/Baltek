Introduction

BALTEK is a turn-based board game, inspired from football, which relies on the following principles:

  • game easily made by hand,
  • with a few rules,
  • without randomizing device,
  • with several actions per turn.
  • The ball moves faster than the footballers.
  • The coordination of footballers is essential.

The name of the game is built with ball (BAL) and technical (TEK) sounds.

Material

Here is the material, shown in the image above:

  • 1 white ball, with the label @.
  • A team of 6 blue footballers.
  • A team of 6 red footballers.
  • Each team consists of:
    • 1 centre forward of force 3.
    • 2 wingers of force 2.
    • 3 fullbacks of force 1.
  • The field is built using 52 green squares.
  • The square a3, with the label #, is the goal defended by the blue team.
  • The square l3, with the label #, is the goal defended by the red team.

The coordinates and labels @ and # are used for notation of moves.

Purpose of the game

  • BALTEK is played by two players.
  • Each player leads a team of 6 footballers.
  • The players decide the assignment of the teams.
  • In turn, each player moves the ball and his/her footballers, trying to send the ball into the opponent's goal.
  • The winner is the player who first scored 2 goals.

Setting up the game

  • At the beginning of the game, as well as after each goal scored, the footballers are set up at their kickoff positions.
  • The kickoff positions are fixed.
  • The kickoff positions of the two teams are symmetrical, except those of the centre forwards.
  • The blue team always makes the first kickoff of the game.
  • The team that has just scored a goal lets the other team kicks off.

Turn of game

Actions, options, compensations, credits and bonuses

  • In turn, each player chooses actions and options, which, depending on the context, require compensations.
  • These choices cost credits or bonuses.
  • Each player receives:
    • 3 credits, at the beginning of each turn;
    • 1 bonus, at each kickoff.
  • In turn, each player can spend 0, 1, 2 or 3 credits, and 0 or 1 bonus.
  • The actions, options and compensations are as follows:
    • Run: moving a footballer; cost: 1 credit.
    • Sprint: option to increase the move of a footballer; cost: 1 bonus.
    • Shoot: move the ball; cost: 1 credit.
    • Duel: compensation of force to own the ball; cost: 1 credit per unit of force.
    • Lob: compensation of force to move the ball; cost: 1 credit per unit of force.

Action run

  • The action run costs 1 credit and allows to move a footballer by 1 square.
  • Each square of the zone 3 x 3, centered on the moved footballer, are possible destinations, unless:
    • it is outside the field;
    • this is a goal square;
    • it already hosts a footballer of the same team.
  • A footballer can not run more than once per turn.
  • The action run does not move the ball.

Option sprint

  • The option sprint costs 1 bonus and extends to 2 squares the move of a footballer.
  • The option sprint must always be combined with the action run.
  • Each square of the 5 x 5 zone, centered on the moved footballer, are possible destinations, unless:
    • it is outside the field;
    • this is a goal square;
    • it already hosts a footballer of the same team.
  • The player can only spend his/her bonus once between two kickoffs.

Example: with yellow borders, the possible destinations for the blue footballer 3.

Action shoot

  • The actions transmit the ball to a footballer, send the ball to an empty square and shoot at the goal obey the same rules.
  • The action shoot costs 1 credit and allows, if the general conditions and the force conditions are satisfied, to move the ball of 1 or 2 squares in horizontal, vertical or diagonal directions, but without curving its trajectory.
  • Here are the general conditions to be met:
    • The player has a shooter in the starting square of the ball.
    • In this turn, the shooter has not already shot.
    • The square of the ball must be in the field.
    • The square of the ball is not the goal defended by the player.
    • The square of the ball may be empty or occupied.
  • Here are the force conditions to be met:
    • If the shooter shares his/ber square with an opponent, then the shooter must:
      • either equal or surpass the force of his/her opponent,
      • or compensate for his/ber force deficit by duel.
    • If the shooter moves the ball over an opponent, then the shooter must:
      • either equal or surpass the force of his/her opponent,
      • or compensate for his/her deficit of force by lob.

Example: with yellow borders, the possible destinations for the ball by the blue shooter 3; none requires duel or lob.

Compensation duel

  • This compensation may be required by the action shoot.
  • The duel compensates for the lack of force of the shooter with respect to the opponent sharing his/her square.
  • The duel costs 1 credit per unit of compensated force.

Examples: to own the ball, the blue shooter 2 must engage in 1 duel; and the blue shooter 1 must engage in 2 duels.

Compensation lob

  • This compensation may be required by the action shoot.
  • The lob compensates for the force deficit of the shooter with respect to the opponent over which the ball passes.
  • The lob costs 1 credit per unit of compensated force.
  • The lob compensation is not necessary to pass over a footballer of his/her team.

Examples: to transmit the ball to the blue footballer 2, the blue shooter 1 must perform 1 lob in the left context and 2 lobs in the right context.

Notation of moves

If useful, the moves of the game are noted as in the following example.

@d3  f3g3 d1e1  / h3g3 g5f5 i3h3
e1e2 d5e4 f5g4  / f5e4 g1f1 i1h2
@e4  @g4+       / g3g4 i5i4 @i4
...
@# /

Optionally, precisions are provided as in the following example.

#! BALTEK 1.1
#@ bernard@bois.fr robert@riviere.fr
#: 2014-12-24 11:09:30
@d3  f3g3 d1e1  / h3g3 g5f5 i3h3
e1e2 d5e4 f5g4  / f5e4 g1f1 i1h2
@e4  @g4+       / g3g4 i5i4 @i4
...
@# /
#: 2014-12-24 11:30:00
# The red bonus has been well played.

Here are the conventions of this format of notation structured in rows:

  • The character, optionally repeated, separates the words.
  • A line beginning with # is a comment or pseudo-comment, and is always optional.
    • # announces a free comment.
    • #! BALTEK i.j announces the version i.j of the BALTEK notation format; usable only once, in the first row.
    • #@ announces the identifiers chosen by the two players, in order blue then red; usable only once.
    • #: announces, using an ISO format, the date, then optionally the time; usable several times.
  • A non-empty line, which is not a comment, describes a blue turn, followed by a red turn.
  • / ends the blue turn. The end of the line ends the red turn .
  • Each turn is composed of 0, 1, 2 ou 3 actions.
    • The action run is noted using one word beginning with the starting square and ending with the arriving square. Example: g3f3
    • For the option sprint, * is appended to the action word. Example: g3e2*
    • The action shot is noted using one word beginning with @ and ending with the arriving square. Example: @c3
    • The shot at goal is abbreviated as follows: @#
    • For the compensation duel, + is appended to the action word. Example: @c3+
    • For two duels, ++ is appended. Example: @c3++
    • For the compensation lob, ! is appended to the action word. Example: @c3!
    • For two lobs, !! is appended to the action word. Example: @c3!!
    • For the combination duel plus lob, +! is appended to the action word. Example: @c3+!

Versioning

  • The versions of the rules are identified with two integer indices i.j.
  • The first version is 1.0.
  • The major index i is incremented by 1 when the logic of the rules changes. The minor index j is then reset to 0.
  • The minor index j is incremented by 1 when the texts or the illustrations or the notation of moves or the structure of the document evolve, but without changing the logic of the rules.
  • Each translation evolves its minor index j from 0.
