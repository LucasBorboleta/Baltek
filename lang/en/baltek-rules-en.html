Introduction

BALTEK is a turn-based board game, inspired from football, which relies on the following principles:

  • game easily made by hand,
  • with a few rules,
  • without random item,
  • with several actions per turn.
  • The ball moves faster than the footballers.
  • The coordination of footballers is essential.

The name of the game is built with ball (BAL) and technical (TEK) sounds.

Material

Here is the material, shown in the image above:

  • 1 white ball, with the label @.
  • A team of 6 blue footballers.
  • A team of 6 red footballers.
  • Each team consists of:
    • 1 striker, of force 3.
    • 2 wingers, of force 2.
    • 3 defenders, of force 1.
  • The field is built using 52 green boxes.
  • Box a3, with the label #, is the goal defended by the blue team.
  • Box l3, with the label #, is the goal defended by the red team.

The coordinates and labels @ and # are used for notation of moves.

Purpose of the game

  • BALTEK is played by two players.
  • Each player leads a team of 6 footballers.
  • The players decide the assignment of the teams.
  • In turn, each player moves the ball and his/her footballers, trying to send the ball into the opponent's goal.
  • The winner is the player who first scored 2 goals.

Setting up the game

  • At the beginning of the game, as well as after each goal scored, the footballers are set up at their kickoff positions.
  • The kickoff positions are fixed.
  • The kickoff positions of the two teams are symmetrical, except those of the strikers.
  • The blue team always makes the first kickoff of the game.
  • The team that has just scored a goal lets the other team kicks off.

Turn of game

Actions, options, compensations, credits and bonuses

  • In turn, each player chooses actions and options, which, depending on the context, require compensations.
  • These choices cost credits or bonuses.
  • Each player has:
    • 3 credits, at the beginning of each turn;
    • 1 bonus, at each kickoff.
  • In turn, each player can spend 0, 1, 2 or 3 credits, and 0 or 1 bonus.
  • The actions, options and compensations are as follows:
    • Run: moving a footballer; cost: 1 credit.
    • Sprint: option to increase the move of a footballer; cost: 1 bonus.
    • Pass/Shoot: move the ball; cost: 1 credit.
    • Duel: compensation of force to own the ball; cost: 1 credit per unit of force.
    • Lob: compensation of force to move the ball; cost: 1 credit per unit of force.

Action run

  • The action run costs 1 credit and allows to move a footballer by 1 box.
  • Each box of the square 3 x 3, centered on the moved footballer, are possible destinations, unless:
    • it is outside the field;
    • this is a goal box;
    • it already hosts a footballer of the same team.
  • A footballer can not run more than once per turn.
  • The action run does not move the ball.

Example: with yellow borders, the possible destinations for the blue footballer 3.

Option sprint

  • The option sprint costs 1 bonus and extends to 2 boxes the move of a footballer.
  • The option sprint must always be combined with the action run.
  • Each box of the 5 x 5 square, centered on the moved footballer, are possible destinations, unless:
    • it is outside the field;
    • this is a goal box;
    • it already hosts a footballer of the same team.
  • The player can only spend his/her bonus once between two kickoffs.

Example: with yellow borders, the possible destinations for the blue footballer 3.

Action pass/shoot

  • The actions pass to a footballer, send on an empty box and shoot at the goal obey the same rules.
  • The action pass/shoot costs 1 credit and allows, if the general conditions and forces are satisfied, to move the ball of 1 or 2 boxes in horizontal, vertical or diagonal directions, but without curving its trajectory.
  • Here are the general conditions to be met:
    • The player has a passer/shooter in the starting box of the ball.
    • In this turn, the passer/shooter has not already passed/shot.
    • The box of the ball must be in the field.
    • The box of the ball is not the goal defended by the player.
    • The box of the ball may be empty or occupied.
  • Here are the conditions of forces to be met:
    • If the passer/shooter shares his/ber box with an opponent, then the passer/shooter must:
      • either equal or surpass the force of his/her opponent,
      • or to compensate for his/ber force deficit by duel.
    • If the passer/shooter moves the ball over an opponent, then the passer/shooter must:
      • either equal or surpass the force of his/her opponent,
      • or compensate for his/her deficit of force by lob.

Example: with yellow borders, the possible destinations for the ball by the blue footballer 3; none requires duel or lob.

Compensation duel

  • Cette compensation peut être exigée par l’action tirer/passer.
  • Le duel compense le déficit de force du passeur/tireur vis-à-vis de l’adversaire qui partage sa case.
  • Le duel coûte 1 crédit par unité de force compensée.

Exemples : pour posséder la balle, le footballeur bleu 2 doit livrer 1 duel ; et footballeur bleu 1 doit livrer 2 duels.

Compensation lob

  • Cette compensation peut être exigée par l’action tirer/passer.
  • Le lob compense le déficit de force du passeur/tireur vis-à-vis de l’adversaire par-dessus lequel passe la balle.
  • Le lob coûte 1 crédit par unité de force compensée.
  • La compensation de lob n'est pas nécessaire pour passer par-dessus un footballeur de son équipe.

Exemples : pour transmettre la balle au footballeur bleu 2, le footballeur bleu 1 doit effectuer 1 lob, dans le contexte de gauche, et 2 lobs dans le contexte de droite.

Notation des coups

Si utile, la partie se note comme dans l'exemple suivant.

@d3  f3g3 d1e1  / h3g3 g5f5 i3h3
e1e2 d5e4 f5g4  / f5e4 g1f1 i1h2
@e4  @g4+       / g3g4 i5i4 @i4
...
@# /

Optionnellement, des précisions sont apportées comme dans l'exemple suivant.

#! BALTEK 1.1
#@ bernard@bois.fr robert@riviere.fr
#: 2014-12-24 11:09:30
@d3  f3g3 d1e1  / h3g3 g5f5 i3h3
e1e2 d5e4 f5g4  / f5e4 g1f1 i1h2
@e4  @g4+       / g3g4 i5i4 @i4
...
@# /
#: 2014-12-24 11:30:00
# Le bonus des rouges a été bien joué.

Voici les conventions de ce format de notation structuré en lignes :

  • Le caractère , optionnellement répété, sépare les mots.
  • Une ligne commençant par # est un commentaire ou pseudo-commentaire, et est toujours optionnel.
    • # annonce un commentaire libre.
    • #! BALTEK i.j annonce la version i.j du format de notation BALTEK ; utilisable une seule fois, en première ligne.
    • #@ annonce les identifiants choisis par les deux joueurs, dans l'ordre bleu, puis rouge ; utilisable une seule fois.
    • #: annonce, dans un format ISO, la date, puis optionnellement l'heure ; utilisable plusieurs fois.
  • Une ligne non-vide, qui n'est pas un commentaire, décrit un tour bleu, suivi d'un tour rouge.
  • / termine le tour bleu. La fin de ligne termine le tour rouge.
  • Chaque tour est composé de 0, 1, 2 ou 3 actions.
    • L'action courir est notée en un mot commençant par la case de départ et finissant par la case d’arrivée. Exemple : g3f3
    • Pour l'option sprint, * est ajouté au mot de l'action. Exemple : g3e2*
    • L’action passer/tirer est notée en un mot commençant par @ et finissant par la case d’arrivée. Exemple : @c3
    • Le tir au but est abrégé par le mot suivant : @#
    • Pour la compensation de duel, + est ajouté au mot de l'action. Exemple : @c3+
    • Pour deux duels, ++ est ajouté. Exemple : @c3++
    • Pour la compensation de lob, ! est ajouté au mot de l'action. Exemple : @c3!
    • Pour deux lobs, !! est ajouté au mot de l'action. Exemple : @c3!!
    • Pour cumuler duel et lob, +! est ajoutée au mot de l'action. Exemple : @c3+!

Versioning

  • The versions of the rules are identified with two integer indices i.j.
  • The first version is 1.0.
  • The major index i is incremented by 1 when the rules of the game are changed. The minor index j is then reset to 0.
  • The minor index j is incremented by 1 when the texts or illustrations or the notation format or the structure of the document change, but not the rules.
  • Each translation evolves its minor index j from 0.
